## Positioning

You are not behind.

You are **pre-trust**.

And in this stage, forcing discovery too early is how you lose the room.

Your job is not to run a diagnostic.

It is to:

```

earn the right to run a diagnostic

```

![Frame](https://img.shields.io/badge/Frame-Context%20Validation-lightgrey?style=flat)

context validation + signal testing

## Objective

![Outcome](https://img.shields.io/badge/Goal-Binary%20Outcome-black?style=flat)

By the end of the call, you want one of two outcomes.

### Outcome A

He says:

```

"Yeah, that actually is a problem for us"

```

→ Move to discovery sprint

---

### Outcome B

You realize:

```

There is no meaningful breakdown

```

→ Exit clean

---

## Opening the Call

![Tone](https://img.shields.io/badge/Tone-Informal-7B8FD4?style=flat)
![Approach](https://img.shields.io/badge/Approach-Story%20Driven-2A3580?style=flat)

Keep it grounded.

No formal tone.

Say:

```

Honestly, I wasn’t trying to turn this into anything heavy.
I just wanted to understand how things actually flow on your side once a deal moves from business development into estimating and then the field.

```

Then:

```

That’s usually where things either stay tight… or start to drift.

```

That line defines your lane.

---

## The Smart Move

![Method](https://img.shields.io/badge/Method-Story%20Pull-black?style=flat)

Do not interrogate.

Pull him into a narrative.

---

### Ask This First

```

Walk me through a recent job. From when you brought it in… to when it actually hit the field.

```

Then stop.

Let him talk.

---

## What You’re Watching For

![Signal](https://img.shields.io/badge/Signal-Behavioral-lightgrey?style=flat)

You are not listening for facts.

You are listening for:

- hesitation  
- vagueness  
- soft language  

Specifically:

- "it depends"  
- "we try to"  
- "usually"  

These are pressure points.

---

## The Targeted Question

![Trigger](https://img.shields.io/badge/Trigger-Ownership%20Test-2A3580?style=flat)

After he explains, ask:

```

When something changes once it’s in the field… who actually owns that call?

```

Then stop talking.

---

## Why This Works

![Effect](https://img.shields.io/badge/Effect-Low%20Resistance-black?style=flat)

You did not:

- interrogate  
- overwhelm  
- pitch  

You:

- let him describe reality  
- surfaced the gap naturally

## If He Engages

![Signal](https://img.shields.io/badge/Signal-Curiosity-7B8FD4?style=flat)

Say:

```

That’s actually where I spend most of my time.
Not tools… just making sure that when things shift, decisions are clear, owned, and actually verified.

```

Pause.

Let him pull you forward.

---

## Introducing the Sprint

![Transition](https://img.shields.io/badge/Transition-Discovery%20Sprint-2A3580?style=flat)

Only if there is signal.

Say:

```

If there’s something there, I usually do a short discovery.
Just map how decisions actually move, where they break, and what needs to be owned differently.

```

Then anchor:

```

It’s not a long engagement. Just enough to know if there’s something worth fixing.

```

## SALT Positioning

![Role](https://img.shields.io/badge/Role-Architecture%20Layer-black?style=flat)

Do not lead with SALT.

Only if asked:

```

If it turns into something bigger, I stay at the architecture layer and bring in a partner team for backend execution.

```

Positioning:

- senior  
- not a vendor  
- not replaceable
