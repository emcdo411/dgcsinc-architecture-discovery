## Notice

This document presents a **working hypothesis** based on common structural patterns observed in construction environments with comparable operating conditions.

It is intentionally designed to be challenged.

> The objective is not agreement.  
> The objective is **clarity under pressure**.

---

## Framing Assumption

```text
Breakdowns do not occur because information is unavailable.
They occur because decision ownership, timing, and verification are unclear when conditions change.
````

---

## Working Hypothesis

D&G Construction Services may be operating within a system where:

* Sales commitments are defined under controlled assumptions
* Field conditions introduce variability not fully captured at the point of sale
* Adjustments occur, but ownership and timing vary by project
* Outcomes are not consistently verified against the original intent

This does not indicate a failure of capability.

It indicates potential **misalignment between decision flow and execution reality**.

---

## Stress Test Questions

The following questions are designed to be answered from direct operational experience.

They are intentionally specific.

---

### Question 1 — Breakdown Point Identification

```text
Where do projects most consistently begin to diverge from the original plan after the deal is won?
```

**Clarification:**

* At what stage does the first meaningful deviation typically occur?
* Is that deviation expected, tolerated, or considered an exception?
* Does the organization treat this as a known condition or an isolated issue?

**Signal Interpretation:**

* Consistent answers indicate pattern awareness
* Inconsistent answers suggest localized handling without systemic visibility

---

### Question 2 — Decision Path Under Change

```text
When a material change occurs in the field, how is the decision to adjust actually made?
```

**Clarification:**

* What triggers recognition that a change requires action?
* Who evaluates the impact and determines the response?
* Is there a defined pathway, or does it depend on the project and individuals involved?

**Signal Interpretation:**

* Defined pathways indicate structured response capability
* Variable pathways indicate reliance on individual judgment under pressure

---

### Question 3 — Ownership and Verification

```text
Who owns the adjustment from identification through resolution, and how is the outcome verified?
```

**Clarification:**

* Is ownership assigned to a role or determined situationally?
* What confirms that the adjustment achieved the intended outcome?
* Is verification documented, observed, or assumed?

**Signal Interpretation:**

* Clear ownership with verification indicates execution integrity
* Diffuse ownership without verification indicates elevated risk of drift

---

## Conditional Escalation

If any of the above questions cannot be answered with consistency across:

* Business Development
* Estimating / Preconstruction
* Project Management / Field Execution

then the system may be operating with:

```text
Partial visibility
Variable ownership
Non-deterministic execution outcomes
```

---

## Structural Indicators

The following indicators typically accompany the above conditions:

* Repeated friction between sales and operations
* Change orders that are reactive rather than structured
* Margin variability that cannot be fully explained at the outset
* Project outcomes that depend heavily on individual experience
* Difficulty tracing decisions back to a clear point of ownership

| Criteria Validity    | Assumptions defined at sale may not hold in execution |
| Execution Capability | Teams are able to act, but not always consistently    |
| Execution Guarantee  | Outcomes depend on context rather than system design  |

---

## Interpretation Boundary

This document does not assert that:

* systems are ineffective
* teams are underperforming
* processes are incorrect

It asserts only that:

> **if the above questions produce variable answers, the system may not be consistently aligned with how decisions need to occur under real conditions.**

---

## Engagement Implication

Where variability is confirmed, the opportunity is not immediate implementation.

The opportunity is:

```text
to map decision flow before modifying system architecture
```

This includes:

* identifying ownership gaps
* clarifying trigger conditions
* aligning response pathways
* establishing verification mechanisms

---

## Guiding Principle

```text
A system can produce insight without producing action.
Only decisions that are owned, triggered, and verified produce consistent outcomes.
```
