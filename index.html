90-Day Fractional Engagement Plan

Architecture before tools — governance before deployment

Days 1–30
Phase 1 — Architecture Before Tools Foundation

Objective: Build the data and governance foundation before any AI tool is selected or purchased.

  • Week 1: System of Record Diagnostic — audit every data-generating process; classify each as Structured / Semi-Structured / Unstructured / No Data Capture. Identify the binding constraint (DBL T-classification).
  • Week 2: DARE Signal Confirmation — run full DARE diagnostic against confirmed internal data from discovery. Score all four dimensions against validated evidence, not OSINT proxies.
  • Week 3: CES Governance Architecture Build — define Criteria Validity, Execution Capability, and Execution Guarantee. Name the technology owner. Build the measurement infrastructure.
  • Week 4: Data Readiness Assessment + AI Roadmap Draft — assess historical project data for AI-readiness. Produce a 12-month adoption roadmap with three phases.

Phase 1 Success Criteria

Named technology owner. Defined system of record. Governance framework. Prioritized AI roadmap. No tools purchased yet.

Days 31–60

Objective: Deploy one AI capability where data is cleanest, ROI is most measurable, and adoption friction is lowest.

Prove Me Wrong

Eight structural hypotheses — disprove what you can. What you can't disprove is where the work begins.

H1
DATA ARCHITECTURE GAP
"DGCS does not have a structured, searchable system of record for historical project performance data. Estimating relies primarily on PM experience and informal knowledge transfer."
Disprove By ShowingNamed PM platform with AI integration. Structured project data export capability. Evidence historical cost data informs current estimating.
H2
REPORTING SPEED GAP
"DGCS produces client-facing project status reports manually, on a weekly or on-request cadence. For clients like CBRE and JLL — AI-native organizations — this creates a vendor tier risk."
Disprove By ShowingActive client portal, automated reporting cadence, or named platform producing real-time dashboards for DaVita or CBRE.
H3
GOVERNANCE OWNERSHIP GAP
"There is no named internal owner for technology and AI adoption decisions at DGCS. Technology investments are made reactively, without a defined evaluation framework or roadmap."
Disprove By ShowingNamed internal technology lead or committee. Defined evaluation criteria. Evidence of a structured technology roadmap.
H4
COMPETITIVE WINDOW CLOSING
"DGCS's primary TX competitors are currently in the AI adoption window — building data moats that will compress DGCS's differentiators within 24–36 months if not addressed now."
Disprove By ShowingEvidence that DGCS's primary client base is not yet requiring AI-enhanced vendor capabilities. Evidence that named competitors have not entered the AI adoption window.
H5
HEALTHCARE VERTICAL RISK
"DGCS's DaVita relationship — multi-state, multi-year — is their most valuable client asset and their highest AI adoption risk. DaVita will eventually face GC vendors with digital capability requirements a non-AI-enabled operator cannot meet."
Disprove By ShowingEvidence DaVita has not introduced digital documentation or platform integration requirements into their GC vendor evaluation criteria.
H6
ESTIMATING = HIGHEST ROI ENTRY
"DGCS's 25 years of project cost data represents a proprietary AI training dataset no competitor can replicate. Structured and AI-enabled, this data would produce estimating accuracy improvements that directly protect margin."
Disprove By ShowingEvidence current estimating accuracy is already at industry-best levels. Evidence historical project data is not accessible enough to serve as an AI training set.
H7
EMERGENCY RESPONSE AT RISK
"DGCS's 24/7 emergency response capability is operationally expensive to maintain without AI-assisted dispatch and predictive maintenance. Without AI augmentation, this differentiator becomes a margin drain as scale increases."
Disprove By ShowingEvidence emergency response margins are healthy. Evidence current dispatch and triage model is operationally efficient at current volume and geographic spread.
H8
WINDOW IS NOW
"The optimal window for DGCS to build AI adoption architecture is the next 90 days — before a major client requires it, before a competitor uses it as a lever against DGCS, and before the cost of catch-up exceeds the cost of building correctly."
Disprove By ShowingEvidence primary clients are not yet signaling technology capability requirements. Evidence the TX commercial GC competitive set has not entered the AI adoption window meaningfully.

The Decision

Two questions. Two roles. One 90-day window.

For the Director of Business Development
The question is not whether AI is coming to construction. That decision has been made by the market. The question is whether DGCS builds the architecture correctly — with governance, data readiness, and measurable ROI as the foundation — or purchases tools reactively and joins the 62% of contractors who cannot demonstrate measurable AI results.
For the COO
The question is who owns this decision inside DGCS. If the answer is "no one yet," that is the first thing this engagement fixes. Every other AI decision flows from having a named owner with a defined framework and a 90-day mandate to build what the market will eventually require anyway.
